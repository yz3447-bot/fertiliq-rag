## Query 4

**Latency:** 36404 ms


**Response:**


A low AMH level does not mean infertility; women with levels as low as 1–3 pmol/L have conceived naturally and with IVF. However, a low AMH indicates a lower quantity of recruitable follicles per cycle and a potentially shorter reproductive window before menopause. If you are undergoing IVF, it also means you might experience a reduced response to ovarian stimulation, leading to fewer eggs retrieved per cycle. Women trying to conceive naturally should not interpret low AMH results as futile.

While AMH cannot be raised beyond your genetic follicular endowment, its measured value can be influenced by modifiable factors. Correcting a Vitamin D deficiency to 50–70 ng/mL 25-OH-D has been shown to improve AMH values. Additionally, DHEA supplementation (25–75 mg/day) may be considered for women with diminished ovarian reserve. Always interpret your AMH results in the context of your genetic profile and clinical history.

**Response:**


To maximize conception chances, focus intercourse within your fertile window – the days when sperm can meet a viable egg. Sperm can survive in the female reproductive tract for 3–5 days, while an egg is viable for 12–24 hours after ovulation (Documentation 2024-03-20, Blog 2024-09-01).

While the fertile window is approximately Days 10–17 in a typical 28-day cycle, this varies significantly between individuals and cycles (Documentation 2024-03-20). The most effective strategy is to ensure sperm are present in the reproductive tract *before* ovulation, as intercourse 3–4 days prior can still lead to fertilization (Blog 2024-09-01).

Aim to have sex every other day during your predicted fertile window. For example, in a 30-day cycle with a fertile window of Days 12–18, recommended timing is Day 11, Day 13, and Day 15 (Forum 2024-06-10). The day with the highest conception probability is typically the one just before your predicted LH surge (Forum 2024-06-10). If you are using an ovulation predictor kit (OPK), which detects the LH surge 24–36 hours before ovulation, have intercourse on the day of the LH surge and the day after (Blog 2024-09-01, Forum 2024-06-10).

In oocytes, mitochondrial oxidative damage is also a significant concern. Therefore, your report highlights this genetic predisposition to increased susceptibility.
